Foreword

    The photolysis rates of photolabile trace species in the atmosphere are important parameters of highest interest to all atmospheric scientists. This is especially true for chemical models of the atmosphere, as all chemical reactions are driven directly or indirectly by the sun’s radiation. Photolysis rates depend not only upon the intensity of the actinic flux, but also upon the photophysical and photochemical properties of the absorbing molecules. Together with the quantum yield of the photolysis processes, the absorption spectra govern the photodissociation frequencies. Both parameters are wavelength dependent, whereby absorption cross sections are known to display a temperature dependence and product quantum yields a pressure dependence. Thus for theoretical considerations and for the numerical determination of photolysis rates, exact knowledge of absorption cross sections is necessary.

    Absorption spectra of atmospheric constituents have been gathered and published for many years in books and scientific journals for the use of the scientific community (see for example: JPL Report „Chemical Kinetics and Photochemical Data for Use in Stratospheric Modeling“ or the Report of the Forschungszentrum Jülich „UV/VIS-Absorption Cross Sections and Quantum Yields for Use in Photochemistry and Atmospheric Modeling“). However, there is an acknowledged dearth of spectra in the UV/VIS wavelength region, and their optimum use is often hindered because of the resolutions selected for the published versions.

    Based on ist practical experience in using such spectra to derive trace gas concentrations from satellite data, and in fulfillment of ist mandate to support atmospheric research, the ATMOS User Center (AUC) at DLR’s DFD together with the Max-Planck-Institute for Chemistry, the University of Essen and the Research Center Karlsruhe has found a convenient way to provide easy access to over one thousend relevant spectra.

    The result is the CD-ROM „UV/VIS Spectra of Atmospheric Constituents“ with a hierarchically structured database containing about 1200 sets of data for 130 substances relevant for atmospheric studies, and related information. In order to enable easy usage of the data, software is supplied  on the CD-ROM which enables handling and display routines, and supports the efficient visualization, merging and rescaling of data, whether for publications, modeling or spectroscopy purposes.

    This first generation CD-ROM (Version-1) is considered to be distributed free-of-charge with among interested scientist. We hope that these users will evaluate the database and provide us with valuable suggestions how to improve the current compilation, in order to make it a precious tool to the atmospheric scientists.

    Finally, our ultimate aim within a few years is to make available a compiled database of recommended and evaluated photolytic parameters on a CD-ROM and on Internet, so that all users will be able to digitally retrieve the best available spectra from the literature via the CD-ROM database.
     

Dr. Geert K. Moortgat
Max Planck Institute for Chemistry, Mainz
